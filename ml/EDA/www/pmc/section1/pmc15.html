What to do if "In Control" but Unacceptable?
In control means process is predictable "In Control" only means that the process is predictable in a statistical sense. What do you do if the process is “in control” but the average level is too high or too low or the variability is unacceptable?
Process improvement techniques Process improvement techniques such as
  • experiments
  • calibration
  • re-analysis of historical database
can be initiated to put the process on target or reduce the variability.
Process must be stable Note that the process must be stable before it can be centered at a target value or its overall variation can be reduced.
